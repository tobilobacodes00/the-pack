# The Pack — Positioning Brief

*What it is, who it's for, what it does differently, and the messaging that flows into the landing page.*

---

## The one-liner

**A research team you can watch.** You give The Pack a question — or your own documents — and a team of
AI agents plan it, research it, and write it up **live, in front of you**, with every claim traced to a
source and a hard cap on what it can spend.

> Not an answer you're handed. A research process you steer.

---

## Who it's for

People who do **research they have to stand behind** — where being confidently wrong is worse than being slow:

- **Founders & operators** — market sizing, competitor teardowns, "should we do X" decisions.
- **Analysts & consultants** — due diligence, briefings, topic reviews they'll put their name on.
- **Strategy / BD / ops** — account research, vendor evaluation, catch-up briefings for a meeting.

They all share one trait: **they need to show their sources.**

---

## What concerns them (the pain)

The current tools each fail them in a specific way:

| Their worry | With ChatGPT / Perplexity / a raw agent |
|---|---|
| *"Can I trust this?"* | Confident answers with vague, hallucinated, or unverifiable sources. |
| *"How did it get here?"* | A black box — one answer appears, no visible reasoning or method. |
| *"It went the wrong way."* | No way to steer mid-research; a wrong angle means starting over. |
| *"What will this cost me?"* | Agent tools burn tokens unpredictably — runaway bills, no ceiling. |
| *"It doesn't know MY context."* | Generic web answers; can't reason over your own documents. |
| *"I need a real deliverable."* | A chat transcript, not a document you can send to a client or a boss. |

Hiring a human research analyst solves the trust problem — but it's **slow and expensive**.

---

## How The Pack helps (pain → answer)

- **Trust → provenance.** Every claim in the brief **cites a source you can click and verify**. A critic
  agent (Sentinel) challenges the weakest claim in a **Standoff** before it ships. It says *"I couldn't
  verify this"* instead of padding — honesty by design.
- **Black box → you watch it work.** The whole research runs on a live canvas (the **Territory**): the plan
  forms, scouts search, findings merge, the brief gets written — in real time, not behind a spinner.
- **Wrong angle → you steer.** You **approve the plan** and edit the team before it runs, **answer questions**
  the pack pauses to ask (Holds), **add input** mid-research, and **stop or redirect** at any point.
- **Runaway cost → a hard cap.** You set a **dollar Boundary**; the engine checks projected spend *before
  every call* and **halts at 100%**. You can even **rehearse** a run's cost before committing.
- **Your context → your library.** Drop in PDFs, CSVs, images, even audio/video — the pack researches
  **your documents alongside the web** and cites them too.
- **Real deliverable → forge it.** The brief downloads as **PDF, DOCX, PPTX, XLSX, HTML, or MD**, or ships as
  a **shareable read-only link**.

---

## What it does *differently* (the wedge)

Most AI research tools optimize for **one fast answer**. The Pack optimizes for a **defensible one**. The
four things almost nothing else combines:

1. **Watchable** — the research is a live process you observe and understand, not a hidden one.
2. **Steerable** — a human gate at every decision (plan, questions, budget, redirect).
3. **Cited & challenged** — claims trace to sources, and a critic actively tries to break the weak ones.
4. **Cost-capped** — a real dollar ceiling, enforced before spend, not discovered on the invoice.

**One line for the pitch:** *ChatGPT hands you an answer; The Pack shows you the work, cites it, lets you
steer it, and won't blow your budget.*

---

## How it works (four beats — for the "how" section)

1. **Say it.** Type, speak, or drop a file. Alpha clarifies until there's a real job.
2. **Approve it.** See the plan and the team; set your budget; hit go (or edit the formation first).
3. **Watch it.** Scouts research in parallel, findings merge, weak claims get challenged — live.
4. **Keep it.** A cited brief you can refine, download in any format, or share by link.

## The proof points (drop-in credibility)

- Every claim → a clickable source; unverifiable claims are flagged, not faked.
- A dollar Boundary that **halts before** overspending (warns at 70%, downgrades at 85%, stops at 100%).
- Works fully offline with no keys (deterministic) — and swaps to real models with zero code change.
- Exports to 6 formats; shareable links; full event log (Tracks) with PII redacted.
